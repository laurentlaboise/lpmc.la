The Heartbeat of Lao Cuisine

Laap (also spelled Larb) is the unofficial national dish of Laos and a staple at any celebration. It's a type of minced meat salad, but calling it just a "salad" doesn't do it justice. It's a vibrant explosion of flavors: spicy, sour, salty, and packed with fresh herbs.

What's Inside?

  • Your Choice of Protein: Traditionally made with beef, pork, or chicken, finely minced.
  • Fresh Herbs: A generous mix of mint, cilantro, and green onions.
  • The Kick: Toasted ground sticky rice (Khao Khoua) for a nutty aroma and texture, along with fresh chilies and lime juice.
  • Perfect Pairing: Always served with a basket of warm sticky rice (Khao Niao), which is rolled into small balls by hand and used to scoop up the laap.

It's more than a meal; it's a communal experience. Perfect for refueling after a long ride.

Interactive & Social Dining

Sindad, or Lao BBQ, is a fun and engaging meal best shared with friends. It features a unique dome-shaped grill placed at your table, combining the best of barbecue and hot pot.

How It Works

  • The Grill: The domed top is for grilling slices of marinated meat (like pork or beef), while the trough around the edge is filled with a savory broth.
  • Cook Your Own: You cook the meat on top, letting the delicious juices run down into the broth.
  • The Broth: Add vegetables, glass noodles, and eggs to the broth, creating a rich and flavorful soup as you grill.
  • Dipping Sauces: Served with a variety of spicy and sweet dipping sauces to customize every bite.

Gather around the grill, share stories from the road, and create a meal that's uniquely yours. It's the perfect way to end a day of adventure.

A Taste of Luang Prabang's Heritage

Or Lam is a deeply flavorful and aromatic stew that originates from the Luang Prabang region. It's a complex, slow-cooked dish that showcases unique local ingredients, offering a true taste of Northern Laos.

Key Ingredients

  • The Unique Flavor: The distinctive taste comes from "Sakhan," a woody stem from a local vine that imparts a spicy, numbing sensation similar to a Sichuan peppercorn.
  • Hearty and Rich: Made with dried buffalo or beef, smoky eggplant, and a variety of local vegetables.
  • Aromatic Herbs: Infused with lemongrass, galangal, and other herbs that create a complex and unforgettable aroma.

This isn't just a soup; it's a culinary journey into the heart of Lao tradition. A must-try for any adventurous foodie.
